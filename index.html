No 7+H

As Andover High School approaches a new year of exciting learning and discovering new interests and ideas, a controversy arises. This is not one of great consequence, one that may determine the success of the students beyond high school. The 7+H schedule will be detrimental not only to a student’s learning, but also their personal stress levels. Proponents of the new schedule are willing to risk the mental health of an indeterminate number of students for MCAS test scores and for the sake of rotating classes throughout the day. Now, last time I checked, curbing the mental health and learning of our students to succeed on a state test was quite the opposite of an incomparable idea. After such great lengths of telling students that success matters more than a silly test, what’s changed?

I’m not convinced that the committee has even considered the financial repercussions that will inevitably take place. Advocates of the schedule offer one figure. $800,000. Now look, I completely understand that the expanded budget will take into account new hires to attend to the students who will be crammed like sardines into already bulging classrooms but what sense does that make? We’ve got schools like High Plain and many of the middle schools bursting at the seams taking on more and more students. Wouldn’t that money be better off going towards repairing and constructing new buildings? The town of Andover needs to be more fiscally conservative and work on behalf of all of the students, not none of them. I recommend you vote your conscience this election and to take into account this disaster that will soon strike the school system.
