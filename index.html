No 7+H

Survey

Introduction

The 7+H schedule is a proposed change to the Current Block Schedule. The New Schedule proposes that the 4-block 2-semester be switched to a 8-day rotation with 5 classes a day. However, the current 8 credits will be replaced with 7, and there will be a new block, dubbed the H-Block, replacing the lost credit.

The new schedule has been in development for over 5 years, as a result of the teacher-district contract negotiations. An independent mediator was brought in by the state of Massachusetts due to the teachers disagreeing with their new contract, which required them to teach an extra class without an increase in pay. This mediator sided with the district, making the teachers teach an extra class, but as a stipulation, teachers and the district were required to form a new schedule; together. The 7+H schedule is a direct result of these negotiations.

The 7+H schedule has been badly received by both students and teachers alike. Our current statistics (as of 3/26/17) find 86% of students in dissent of it, with 7% indifferent. 61% of teachers oppose the new schedule. The new changes are not favorable; losing a credit is hard for many, the 8-day rotation is bound to give headaches, and having 7 classes at once? It sounds like a recipe for true disaster, and many students agree.

However, what’s most disheartening with the new schedule is the lack of input from Students. Were the students ever consulted on this issue? It was simply presented to them one day, and they were forced to accept it. Schools are meant for the student, and this pushes against the qualities that make Andover students great; the passion, the drive, and the shared sense of community. The new schedule disrupts all of these conditions.

Ever since the schedule was introduced, there have been many attempts to get it shut down. However, all attempts have unfortunately failed. This time around, we want to stop the schedule, and we are willing to put in the time and effort required. We are a team of 5 dedicated sophomores, who care about our fellow peers and want the best for them.

We hope you support our cause! Feel free to explore the website, where we have gathered resources for your information. Let us know how you feel with the survey.

To Whom it may Concern:

Given the vast popularity of this topic, I will not waste your time with extraneous paragraphs with excessive explanations of numerous reasons that the 7+H block schedule is malicious to our school system. Rather, I will be focusing this letter on the an effect of the implementation of the new schedule that is overlooked by students. Quite simply: we, as a town, cannot afford this schedule. To support the required staff, textbooks, and other materials to allow for year-long courses that run concurrently, the schools would need to appropriate additional funds, somewhere in the range of one million dollars. One million dollars that, in all likelihood, will not be added to the school’s annual budget.

Where then will the money to support this schedule come from? The arts. We have seen this time and time again whenever money is needed elsewhere in the schools. Courses in the visual and performing arts get reduced or removed completely. Full credit courses become half credit become nonexistent. Funds to support the programs and materials are taken from an already financially struggling department to support a schedule that the student body of Andover High School has such strong feelings against.

The implementation of this schedule requires students to take a free block, as opposed to the optional study students may opt in or opt out of with the current 2 day block rotation schedule. While this gives students time to work with their teachers, it deprives them of the ability to take an additional half credit course. As most of the courses in the arts are half credit courses, not only will this schedule be depriving the arts the money to support their curriculums, but fewer students will have the option to take the courses offered. With the current schedule, students still have time to work with their instructors in a study, if they choose to take one. It does not, however, require students to take a break period. Students may fill their schedule completely with courses if they prefer the additional credit.

If Andover schools truly did honor the voices of their students, this schedule would not be implemented. The overwhelming protest of the schedule by students, teachers, and parents alike are ignored by the administration that continues on with the H block schedule. If we still believe in an earnest democracy, we will not just request from the school board, but demand from our elected officials to protect the beliefs of our students, and overrule the choice of the High School administration for the use of the 7+H block schedule.
