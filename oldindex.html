Introduction

The 7+H schedule is a proposed change to the Current Block Schedule. The New Schedule proposes that the 4-block 2-semester be switched to a 8-day rotation with 5 classes a day. However, the current 8 credits will be replaced with 7, and there will be a new block, dubbed the H-Block, replacing the lost credit.

The new schedule has been in development for over 5 years, as a result of the teacher-district contract negotiations. An independent mediator was brought in by the state of Massachusetts due to the teachers disagreeing with their new contract, which required them to teach an extra class without an increase in pay. This mediator sided with the district, making the teachers teach an extra class, but as a stipulation, teachers and the district were required to form a new schedule; together. The 7+H schedule is a direct result of these negotiations.

The 7+H schedule has been badly received by both students and teachers alike. Our current statistics (as of 3/26/17) find 86% of students in dissent of it, with 7% indifferent. 61% of teachers oppose the new schedule. The new changes are not favorable; losing a credit is hard for many, the 8-day rotation is bound to give headaches, and having 7 classes at once? It sounds like a recipe for true disaster, and many students agree.
